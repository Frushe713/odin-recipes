Katsudon: japanese deep-fried breaded pork cutlet

Description:

Katsudon (Japanese: カツ丼) is a popular Japanese food, a bowl of rice topped with a deep-fried breaded pork cutlet, egg, vegetables, and condiments.

The dish takes its name from the Japanese words tonkatsu(for pork cutlet) and donburi (for rice bowl dish).

Ingredients:

  • Pork loin 180g
  • 2 eggs
  • Some plain flour
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Bag of panko
  • Salt to taste
  • 100ml of water
  • Kombu
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 4 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sake
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin
  • Half an onion
  • 5 grams of ginger
  • 1/2 cup of rice(per person)

Steps:

  1. Take skin off the pork and flatten it with the back of the knife
  2. Cut onion against the grain
  3. Mince your ginger
  4. Mix 1/3 tablespoon of kombu salt and 1/6 tablespoon of sugar and sprinkle it on both sides of the pork
  5. Leave the pork outside your fridge for it to reach room temperature for 0.5-1 hour
  6. Soak kombu and water for 1 hour (best to do it overnight)
  7. Mix in water, kombu, sugar, soy sauce, sake and mirin
  8. Mix in your panko with plain flour and black pepper
  9. Wet your pork with egg and crumb with your panko mixture
  10. Cook your sauce
  11. Add oil to a cold pan with ginger (medium heat)
  12. Once your ginger starts to smell fragant add your onion
  13. Add a pinch of salt
  14. Once you see your onion soften add the sauce you previously made
  15. Boil for 30 seconds and then turn off your heat
  16. Wait a few minutes for the sauce to infuse the flavors
  17. Deep fry the pork at 180-183°C
  18. Cook for 2.5 mins and take out to rest for 2 mins
  19. Meanwhile, reheat the sauce and discard the kombu
  20. Add the fried pork to the sauce
  21. Serve on top of rice
  22. Whisk 2 eggs and make scrambled eggs then serve on pork
  23. Enjoy!
